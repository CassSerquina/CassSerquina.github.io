With ‘AI slop’ distorting our reality, the world is sleepwalking into disaster : Nesrine Malik

"My opinion on AI and AI generation as a whole has always been pretty negative since its first appearance on the internet. Back then when AI was in its infancy it started off not very useful on the side of image generation but as years went by its rapid evolution and impact on the media and these social platforms made it apparent that AI is going to become very common in the near future. Seeing this rapid improvement of AI in just a few years made a lot of people worry including myself. At what point do AI images become almost unrecognizable? And what impact do these images have on society as a whole? It is a very scary thought. AI content has already become commonplace in social media platforms like X and Instagram. While many discourage and condemn AI images especially in the art community many have also begun incorporating it into art. These “AI artists” are very much present sharing these AI generated images and passing it off of their own work. The worst part is that some of them condemn and look down on artists in which they often take their work and run it through AI to get “fixed”. One point that caught my attention was when Nesrine Malik makes a point that due to the rise of AI images in media, the constant exposure to these images would ultimately lead to desensitization and confusion. Social media platforms like Instagram often feed users with AI slop content since it is not only cheap but engaging as well. Users often go to this for profit even if the information that comes from the post is misleading. The newer generation is very much susceptible to this type of content as it is present through many youtube videos, even ones dedicated to children. I caught my brother one day watching an AI slop video and naturally told him to stop watching it or else I'd take his ipad away. To describe the video the colors were very saturated, the audio was incoherent and very loud, and the editing style of the video was purposely made very fast and jumpy. I'm afraid that the newer generation would fall into this type of AI slop content and because of that soon become dependent on it for entertainment. My attention span has already become pretty bad due to the addicting nature of instagram reels and the content present within today's media. Just imagining AI slop videos mixed in with the already addicting nature of instagram reels would very much destroy one's attention span and may cause problems as they grow up. I feel AI as a whole has become too much for our own good. AI has advanced so much in so little time that society as a whole can't keep up with it. With the rise in AI, many large companies are starting to incorporate it into their services which is a horrible sign. Google with its AI summary, Coca-Cola and many gaming studios using AI art for promotion and in game items, gaming companies using voice actors to train AI, etc. I feel society should not blindly advance AI without any policies put in place to limit its usage, because we continue to advance AI without any precautions in mind its harmful impact would very much be felt. "
