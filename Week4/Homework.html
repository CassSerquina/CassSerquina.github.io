Explanation

This photomontage speaks to the uncertainty of choices present within life as a whole. From the wide variety of factors that come with making a choice to the procrastination that falls from even deciding one. Whether that choice is wrong or right is never known unless you take that choice and step through that door. I imagined this image as a sort of purgatory environment where you sit, contemplate, and decide on what the “correct” option would be.This a place of discussion and action where you would simply imagine the outcomes of that choice and a place where you discuss with either something or someone which choice is correct (as shown by the table the two chairs).

To the techniques I used, I played with the perspective of the doors rearranging them to fit the background image of the sky. I edited the Hue and saturation of the doors trying my best to match them as many of the images I chose varied in whites from warm to cool. For the foreground I added a contrast layer above the original image as I wanted it to pop out from the background. I also purposely moved the overlay layer to not match the original position to give it that surreal and dream like feeling. For the doors I also added a multiply layer to further contrast the doors from the foreground. For the background I chose a yellowish sky in which I overlayed it making it more saturated but darker as well.
